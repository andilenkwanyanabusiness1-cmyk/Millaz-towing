Understanding Towing Regulations in KZN

18 February 2026 5 min read

A Patient Guide to Your Rights on the Road

Navigating the legal landscape of towing in South Africa can be as stressful as the accident itself. In KwaZulu-Natal, where multiple municipalities have specific by-laws, motorists often find themselves overwhelmed by regulatory complexity. This guide demystifies the National Road Traffic Act and local KZN regulations, ensuring you know exactly what is legal, what is optional, and how to protect your interests.

The Legal Right of Refusal

Perhaps the most misunderstood regulation in South Africa is the motorist's right to choose their service provider. Unless your vehicle is blocking a major arterial route (like the N3 or M4) and creating an immediate traffic hazard, the South African Police Service (SAPS) and Metro Police MUST allow you to contact your own insurance-approved or preferred towing company.

You are under no legal obligation to use the first tow truck that arrives on the scene. Despite aggressive marketing from some "first responders," the choice of who tows your vehicle rests with you, provided the scene doesn't pose an immediate public safety risk.

Consumer Right: You have the right to request a formal quote and an "Authority to Tow" form before your vehicle is hooked up. Never allow a vehicle to be moved without a signed document specifying the destination and estimated costs.

Regulatory Storage and Towing Rates

In South Africa, there is currently no national regulated flat rate for towing, which leads to significant price variance. However, high-quality providers adhere to the guidelines set by major insurance groups and the United Towing Association of South Africa (UTASA).

Storage fees are another area of regulatory contention. Under common law, a towing company has a "lien" (ledger of right) over the vehicle until towing and storage costs are paid. However, these costs must be reasonable and market-related. In KZN, many reputable companies, including Millaz Towing, offer transparent, insurance-matched rates to prevent storage fee disputes.

Police-Directed Tows vs. Private Tows

There are instances where a police officer may order a vehicle to be removed by a specific contract tower. This usually happens if the driver is incapacitated, the vehicle is evidence in a crime, or it's causing a major traffic obstruction. Even in these cases, you (or your family) have the right to request that the vehicle be moved to a secure, verified facility rather than an unknown yard.

Verification Check: Safe Towing Protocols

Under KZN by-laws, every recovery vehicle must be clearly marked and the driver must carry identification. Before agreeing to a tow, check for:

  • Clearly visible company branding and contact details.
  • Valid trade plates and registration.
  • Standard safety equipment (beacon lights, triangles).

The Bottom Line

Knowledge is your best defense against predatory practices. By understanding that you have the right to choose your provider, the right to a transparent quote, and the right to verified storage, you can manage a vehicle recovery with confidence and legal clarity.
