Emergency Road Safety Tips

18 February 2026 5 min read

Preparing for the Unexpected

Road safety isn't just about how you drive; it's about how you prepare for the moments when things go wrong. In South Africa, roadside emergencies can range from a simple flat tire to complex multi-vehicle collisions. This guide provides practical, life-saving tips to help you manage emergency situations with confidence and stay safe until professional help arrives.

The Essential Emergency Kit

Your safety begins long before you turn the ignition. Every vehicle should be equipped with a basic emergency kit. At a minimum, this should include:

  • A fully inflated spare tire and a functioning jack and lug wrench.
  • A high-visibility reflective vest (to be worn whenever you exit the vehicle).
  • At least two reflective warning triangles.
  • A basic first-aid kit with bandages, antiseptic, and gloves.
  • A portable power bank for your mobile phone.
  • A bottle of water and a warm blanket (especially for winter travel).

Visibility: Your First Line of Defense

If you are forced to stop on the roadside, visibility is your most important asset. Activate your hazard lights immediately. If it's night or visibility is poor, keep your interior lights on as well to make your vehicle more conspicuous. Remember, high-speed traffic needs significant time and distance to react to a stationary object.

Safety Rule: Never attempt to change a tire on the side of a vehicle exposed to live traffic. It is better to damage your rim by driving slowly to a safer location than to risk being struck by a passing car.

Handling Mechanical Failure

If your engine begins to overheat or notice a sudden mechanical problem, don't wait for the car to stop. Signal and move to the far-left shoulder immediately. If you are on a highway, try to reach the next exit or a designated service area. If you must stop on the shoulder, turn your wheels toward the grass/barrier rather than the road.

Calling for Help

Ensure you have the contact details for your insurance roadside assistance and a trusted local recovery service like Millaz Towing saved in your phone. When calling for help, be prepared to provide your exact location, the nature of the problem, and whether any passengers have special needs (e.g., medical conditions or young children).

The Bottom Line

Emergency road safety is about minimizing risk through preparation and smart decision-making. By maintaining your emergency kit, prioritizing visibility, and knowing exactly who to call, you can turn a potentially dangerous situation into a manageable one.
