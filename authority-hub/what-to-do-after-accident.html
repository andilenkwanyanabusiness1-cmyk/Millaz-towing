What to Do After a Road Accident

18 February 2026 5 min read

Clear Steps for Stressful Situations

An accident is an overwhelming experience that triggers your "fight or flight" response, making clear thinking difficult. Whether it's a major collision or a minor bump, having a pre-determined sequence of actions is vital. This guide provides a definitive checklist for South African motorists to ensure safety, legal compliance, and successful insurance claims processing.

Step 1: Stop and Secure

The National Road Traffic Act requires you to stop immediately after any collision. Failing to do so is a serious criminal offense. Activate your hazard lights and, if safe to do so, move your vehicle to the shoulder of the road to prevent secondary impacts. Place your warning triangle at least 45 meters behind the scene.

Step 2: Check for Injuries

Systematically check yourself and your passengers for injuries. If anyone is injured, call 10177 or 112 from your mobile phone immediately. Do not move an injured person unless there is an immediate threat to their life (like fire), as this can worsen spinal injuries.

Note: If anyone is killed or injured, you must remain at the scene until a police officer tells you that you may leave.

Step 3: Exchange Information

By law, you must exchange details with the other parties involved. Collect the following:

  • Full names and ID numbers.
  • Contact numbers and physical addresses.
  • Vehicle registration numbers.
  • Insurance company details and policy numbers.

Step 4: Document the Evidence

Before any vehicles are moved (if possible and safe), take clear photographs of the entire scene. Capture the position of all vehicles, damage close-ups, road signs, and any skid marks. This visual evidence is invaluable during insurance disputes and police investigations.

Step 5: Report to SAPS

If no one is injured and no public property is damaged, you may not need to wait for the police at the scene. However, you MUST report the accident at a police station within 24 hours (or as soon as reasonably possible if hospitalized). Ensure you receive an Accident Report (AR) number, as your insurance company will require this to process your claim.

The Bottom Line

The minutes following an accident are chaotic, but following this sequence ensures you meet your legal obligations and protect your rights. Stay calm, document everything, and contact a professional recovery service to manage your vehicle once the administrative tasks are complete.
