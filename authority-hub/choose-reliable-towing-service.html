How to Choose a Reliable Towing Service

18 February 2026 5 min read

More Than Just a Hook and a Winch

When you're stranded on the side of a busy KZN road, your primary goal is to get home safely and ensure your vehicle is handled professionally. However, not all towing services are created equal. Choosing the wrong provider can lead to excessive costs, vehicle damage, and unnecessary stress. This guide outlines the essential criteria for selecting a reliable vehicle recovery partner.

Accreditation and Professionalism

The first thing to look for is industry accreditation. In South Africa, reputable companies are typically members of organizations like the United Towing Association of South Africa (UTASA) or are approved by major insurance groups. Accreditation means the company adheres to a set of ethical standards, safety protocols, and transparent pricing structures.

Equipment and Versatility

A professional towing company should have a fleet capable of handling various scenarios. Whether you drive a compact hatchback, a luxury SUV, or a heavy-duty commercial vehicle, the recovery team should have the right equipment (like flatbeds or specialized wheel-lift systems) to move your car without causing secondary damage. Ask if they use soft-strap tie-downs to protect your vehicle's suspension and finish.

Pro Tip: Look for a company that offers a range of services, including basic roadside assistance, long-distance towing, and specialized recovery. This versatility is often a sign of a well-established and professional operation.

Insurance Partnerships

Most South African insurance policies include roadside assistance for accidents and mechanical breakdowns. A reliable towing service will have established relationships with major insurance providers (like OUTsurance, Old Mutual, or Santam). This often means your tow might be fully covered, and the provider will handle the complex paperwork on your behalf.

Transparent Pricing

Predatory pricing is a major concern in the towing industry. A reliable company will provide a clear, written estimate before starting any work. They should be upfront about their hook-up fees, per-kilometer rates, and any potential storage costs. Avoid companies that are vague about their pricing or demand immediate cash payments at the scene.

24/7 Availability and Response Times

Emergencies don't happen on a 9-to-5 schedule. A professional service like Millaz Towing operates 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, every day of the year. When you call, they should be able to give you an accurate estimated time of arrival (ETA) based on their current dispatch positions.

The Bottom Line

Don't wait until you're in an emergency to find a towing company. Do your research now, check online reviews, and save the number of a verified provider who prioritizes safety, transparency, and professional service. A little preparation today can save you significant trouble tomorrow.
